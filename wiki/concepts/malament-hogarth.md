# Malament-Hogarth Spacetimes

## Gödel's Discovery

In 1949, Kurt Gödel presented Einstein with a solution to general relativity that allows closed timelike curves (CTCs) — paths that loop back in time. Einstein's response was that the model is mathematically valid but physical reasons might exclude it. More than 75 years later, no knockdown argument has been found.

The Gödel solution shows that time travel in GR is not changing the past (Back to the Future style) but following a worldline that wraps back on itself, revisiting the same event.

## Malament's Contribution

David Malament (University of California, Irvine) pioneered the rigorous study of causal structure in GR. His key results:

1. **Causal structure determines topology** (Malament 1977): For spacetimes with sufficiently rich causal structure, the causal structure uniquely determines the topology. This is foundational for the causal set approach to quantum gravity.

2. **Rotation in GR is ambiguous**: Malament formalized multiple definitions of rotation in GR and proved they are inequivalent — an object can be rotating by one criterion but not another.

3. **Minimal causal structure**: Malament identified the minimal causal structure needed to determine topology. In time travel universes where every point is causally related to every other point, the theorem fails.

## Malament-Hogarth Spacetimes

Malament-Hogarth (MH) spacetimes are spacetimes that allow an observer to witness an infinite amount of time in their past while experiencing only finite time themselves. These are necessarily not globally hyperbolic.

Key properties:
- Allow hypercomputation (solving the halting problem via spacetime)
- David Malament conceived the idea but never published it — credit goes to his correspondence with John Earman and Mark Hogarth's first publication
- The main theorem (MH spacetimes are not globally hyperbolic) is Malament's

## Connection to Time Travel

Malament-Hogarth spacetimes represent a different kind of time travel: not revisiting past events, but accessing infinite computational resources through causal structure manipulation. This connects to the [[time-travel-machinery]] concept where spacetime curvature enables non-standard information processing.

## Significance

Malament's approach — making philosophical questions precise using GR formalism and proving theorems — is a model for rigorous foundations of physics. His work shows that time travel in GR is not science fiction but a serious mathematical question with no easy resolution.
