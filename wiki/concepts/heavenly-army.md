# Heavenly Army

## The Vision

The Heavenly Army is described in 2 Kings 6:15-17 and Zechariah 1:8-17 as a celestial army of horsemen and chariots of fire.

## Description

2 Kings 6:15-17 describes:

- "Elisha's servant saw the mountains full of 'horses and chariots of fire'"
- The servant was afraid, but Elisha prayed for his eyes to be opened
- "And the Lord opened the eyes of the young man, and he saw. And behold, the mountain was full of horses and chariots of fire all around Elisha"

Zechariah 1:8-17 describes:

- "I saw by night, and behold, a man riding among the myrtle trees in the hollow"
- "The man who stood among the myrtle trees responded"
- "I saw four horns" and "four craftsmen"

## Modern Interpretation

UAP researchers have interpreted the Heavenly Army as a description of celestial beings and their craft:

- **Chariots of fire**: Metallic craft with lights
- **Horsemen**: Beings riding in craft
- **Mountains full**: Multiple craft in the sky
- **Divine presence**: The beings are divine or celestial

## Relevance to UFOs

The Heavenly Army is one of the earliest descriptions of a celestial army. It shares many features with modern UAP reports:

- **Chariots of fire**: Metallic craft with lights
- **Horsemen**: Beings riding in craft
- **Mountains full**: Multiple craft in the sky
- **Divine presence**: The beings are divine or celestial
