# Second Law of Thermodynamics

## The Law

The Second Law of Thermodynamics states that the entropy of an isolated system never decreases: $\Delta S \geq 0$.

## The Meaning

The Second Law is the most fundamental law of physics. It explains why time flows forward, why eggs break, why heat flows from hot to cold, and why the universe is evolving toward a state of maximum entropy.

## The Implications

1. **Time's arrow**: Time flows forward because entropy increases.
2. **Irreversibility**: Most processes are irreversible (you can't unscramble an egg).
3. **Heat death**: The universe will eventually reach maximum entropy (heat death).
4. **Information**: Information is physical — erasing information costs energy (Landauer).
5. **Maxwell's demon**: Information can be used to decrease entropy (but the demon pays its thermodynamic debt).

## The Connection to Time Travel

Time travel is entropy reconfiguration. If entropy is a property of the field (not absolute), then time travel is reconfiguring the field so entropy flows "backward."
