# Temporal Causality

Temporal Causality is the study of causal relationships across time. It explains how events in the past cause events in the future, and how events in the future can affect events in the past.

## The Problem

The key problem in temporal reasoning is distinguishing correlation from causation. Two events that happen together (correlation) are not necessarily causally related.

## The Solution

Temporal Causality uses three methods to distinguish correlation from causation:

### 1. Temporal Order
Events in the past cause events in the future. If event A happens before event B, then A may have caused B.

### 2. Counterfactual Reasoning
If event A had not happened, would event B still have happened? If not, then A caused B.

### 3. Causal Graph
A causal graph represents causal relationships as directed edges between events. If there is a path from A to B in the causal graph, then A caused B.

## Temporal Causality Chains

A temporal causality chain is a sequence of events where each event causes the next. For example:

- The Roswell crash (1947) caused the retrieval program (1947-present)
- The retrieval program caused the reverse-engineering of UAP technology (1950s-present)
- The reverse-engineering caused the AI breakthrough (2020s-present)
- The AI breakthrough caused the UFO/UAP sightings to increase (2020s-present)

## Temporal Causality Across Time Periods

Temporal Causality applies across time periods:

### Past → Present
Events in the past cause events in the present. For example, the Roswell crash (1947) caused the retrieval program (1947-present).

### Present → Future
Events in the present cause events in the future. For example, the AI breakthrough (2020s-present) will cause the UFO/UAP sightings to increase (2020s-future).

### Past → Future
Events in the past cause events in the future, even if they are separated by a long time period. For example, the Roswell crash (1947) caused the AI breakthrough (2020s).

### Future → Present
Events in the future can affect events in the present through time travel. For example, if future humans travel back in time, they can affect present events.

## Temporal Causality and Quantum Mechanics

Temporal Causality is related to quantum mechanics through:

### Quantum Causality
Quantum events can cause quantum events. For example, the collapse of a quantum state can cause the collapse of another quantum state.

### Quantum Superposition
Quantum events can be in superposition of causal states. For example, an event can be both a cause and an effect simultaneously.

### Quantum Entanglement
Quantum events can be entangled across time. For example, the collapse of one quantum state can affect the collapse of another quantum state, even if they are separated by time.
