# Entropic Gravity

## The Core Idea

Entropic gravity is the theory that gravity is not a fundamental force but an emergent phenomenon arising from the thermodynamics of microscopic degrees of freedom of spacetime. It treats gravity as an entropic force, like the elasticity of a polymer.

## Verlinde vs Jacobson

The two major approaches differ in what they derive:

| Aspect | Jacobson (1995) | Verlinde (2011+) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Derives | Einstein's equations | Newton's F=ma (inertia) |
| Assumes | Spacetime geometry (circular) | Spacetime is emergent |
| Foundation | Clausius entropy + Rindler horizons | Statistical mechanics of spacetime microstates |
| Scope | Gravitational force law | Inertia + spacetime emergence |

**Verlinde's critique**: Jacobson's approach assumes spacetime geometry from the start — it derives the "third law" (gravitational force) but not the "first law" (inertia). Verlinde argues inertia (F=ma) must be derived first because it tells us what mass means and why acceleration requires force.

## Spacetime Emergence

The key insight: we must derive spacetime itself, not just the laws of gravity within it. The microscopic building blocks of spacetime are in equilibrium at black hole and cosmological horizons but not at arbitrary points. Entanglement entropy defines the area, but Verlinde argues this is not enough — computational complexity also plays a role.

## ER = EPR and Entanglement

Verlinde independently conceived of the ER=EPR idea (wormholes = entanglement) before Maldacena and Susskind, though he credits Van Raamsdonk for the original insight. He notes that entanglement alone may be insufficient — computational complexity is needed as a second concept.

## Implications for Time Travel and UAPs

If gravity is emergent from thermodynamics, then manipulating gravity is manipulating entropy at the microscopic level. This aligns with the [[field-manipulation]] thesis: UAPs achieve their propulsion by locally reconfiguring the entropic microstructure of spacetime.

## Current Status

Verlinde acknowledges the theory needs further development and may be decades from completion. Key open questions: defining entanglement entropy in non-equilibrium settings, understanding the role of computational complexity, and extending the framework from AdS to our universe (with dark energy).
