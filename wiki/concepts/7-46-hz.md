# 7.46 Hz — The UFO Frequency

7.46 Hz is a specific electromagnetic frequency that appears in multiple contexts related to UAPs, consciousness, and the Earth's natural resonance.

## The Schumann Resonance Connection

The Earth's ionosphere acts as a resonant cavity, and the first Schumann resonance is typically cited as 7.83 Hz. However, several sources — including UFO researcher Christopher B. Freedman — cite 7.46 Hz as a specific frequency associated with UAP phenomena. The difference between 7.46 Hz and 7.83 Hz may reflect different models of the ionosphere or different aspects of the resonance.

## Connection to Element 115

Bob Lazar claims that Element 115 generates an intense gravitational field when energized. Several sources suggest that Element 115 resonates at or near 7.46 Hz, making this frequency a key parameter for understanding how alien craft propulsion works. The frequency may be the "carrier frequency" at which the exotic matter interacts with normal matter.

## Connection to Consciousness

7.46 Hz falls in the theta range of brain waves (4–8 Hz), which is associated with:
- Deep meditation and altered states of consciousness
- Creativity and insight
- The boundary between the conscious and subconscious mind
- The "abduction" experience

This aligns with the wiki's exotic-matter-and-consciousness theory, which proposes that exotic matter is the substrate of consciousness. If UAPs use exotic matter for propulsion, they may also use it for consciousness-related processes.

## Connection to Field Manipulation

The field-manipulation theory of UAP propulsion suggests that UAPs manipulate electromagnetic and gravitational fields to achieve their flight characteristics. 7.46 Hz may be the frequency at which these fields are most effectively coupled, allowing the craft to achieve:
- Hovering without sonic booms
- Rapid acceleration and deceleration
- Vertical takeoff and landing
- High-speed travel

## Connection to UFO Retrieval

David Grusch's 2023 testimony has been linked to the 7.46 Hz frequency. Some researchers believe that the recovered UAPs operate at this frequency, and that the frequency is a key signature for identifying UAP activity.
