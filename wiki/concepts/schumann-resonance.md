# Schumann Resonance

The Schumann resonance is a set of peaks in the extremely low frequency (ELF) portion of the Earth's electromagnetic spectrum, generated by lightning discharges in the cavity between the Earth's surface and the ionosphere.

## The First Schumann Resonance

The first Schumann resonance is typically cited as 7.83 Hz, with higher harmonics at approximately 14.3 Hz, 20.8 Hz, 27.3 Hz, and 33.8 Hz. These resonances are generated by the continuous electrical activity of thunderstorms around the world.

## Connection to 7.46 Hz

Some researchers cite 7.46 Hz as the frequency associated with UAP phenomena. The difference between 7.46 Hz and 7.83 Hz may reflect:
- Different models of the Earth's ionosphere
- Different aspects of the resonance (fundamental vs. dominant frequency)
- The specific frequency of UAP propulsion systems vs. the natural Schumann resonance

## Connection to UFOs

- **Element 115** — Bob Lazar claims Element 115 resonates near the Schumann frequency
- **Consciousness** — The Schumann resonance is in the theta range, associated with deep meditation and altered states of consciousness
- **Field manipulation** — The resonance may be the natural frequency at which UAPs interact with the Earth's electromagnetic field
- **UFO frequency** — 7.46 Hz is sometimes called the "UFO frequency" in UFO research

## Connection to the Earth

The Schumann resonance is a natural phenomenon that connects the Earth to the broader electromagnetic environment. This supports the wiki's earth-as-space-craft theory, which proposes that the Earth itself is a point of entanglement for everything in the universe.
