# Black Hole Entropy

## Bekenstein-Hawking Entropy

Bekenstein (1973) and Hawking (1974) discovered that black holes have entropy proportional to their surface area, not their volume:

$$S = \frac{k_B c^3 A}{4 G \hbar}$$

where $A$ is the surface area of the event horizon.

## The Discovery

This was revolutionary because entropy should scale with volume, yet black holes suggest a deeper connection between area and information. Black holes are the most information-dense objects in the universe.

## Hawking Radiation

Hawking (1974) showed that black holes emit thermal radiation and slowly evaporate. The temperature is inversely proportional to mass. This proved that black holes are not truly black and that gravity, quantum mechanics, and thermodynamics are deeply intertwined.

## The Information Paradox

The information paradox (Hawking, 1976) is the question of whether information is lost when matter falls into a black hole. The resolution (largely accepted) is that information is preserved in subtle correlations in Hawking radiation.

## The Holographic Principle

Bekenstein-Hawking entropy foreshadowed the holographic principle: the universe may be a projection of information encoded on a surface.

## The Connection to UAPs

Black holes are natural entropy sinks. If UAPs harness exotic matter (negative energy), they may be manipulating black-hole-like entropy gradients.
