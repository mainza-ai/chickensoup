# Biefeld-Brown Effect

The Biefeld-Brown effect (also called the Biefeld-Brown reaction or electrogravitics) is a phenomenon observed in asymmetric capacitors subjected to high-voltage electric fields.

## Discovery

In the 1920s, Thomas Townsend Brown and Paul Biefeld discovered that when a capacitor with one small plate and one large plate is charged to a high voltage, it experiences a force toward the smaller plate. This thrust cannot be fully explained by conventional ion wind and suggests a direct coupling between electricity and gravity.

## Mechanism

The effect is thought to arise from:
- **Ion wind** — Partially explains the thrust in air
- **Electrogravitic coupling** — A direct interaction between the electric field and the gravitational field, which cannot be explained by ion wind alone
- **Field manipulation** — The asymmetric capacitor creates a localized distortion in the gravitational field

## Connection to UFOs and Time Travel

- **Antigravity propulsion** — The Biefeld-Brown effect provides a mechanism for how UAPs achieve their remarkable flight characteristics
- **Element 115** — Bob Lazar claims that Element 115 generates an intense gravitational field, and Brown's work suggests similar mechanisms at work in UAP propulsion
- **Field manipulation** — The effect demonstrates that electric fields can manipulate gravity, a key requirement for the field-based time travel theory
