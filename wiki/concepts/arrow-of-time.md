# Arrow of Time

## The Core Problem

The arrow of time is the question of why time flows in one direction. The microscopic laws of physics are time-reversible, yet macroscopic processes are not.

## The Thermodynamic Arrow

The thermodynamic arrow of time is the most fundamental: time flows forward because entropy increases. The Second Law states that entropy of an isolated system never decreases.

## The Past Hypothesis

The Past Hypothesis (Albert, 2000) is the claim that the universe began in an extraordinarily low-entropy state (the Big Bang). This is the deepest unsolved question in cosmology.

## Loschmidt's Paradox

Loschmidt's paradox (1876) is the apparent contradiction between the time-reversibility of microscopic laws and the irreversibility of macroscopic processes. The resolution is that the arrow is statistical, not absolute: entropy doesn't have to increase; it's overwhelmingly likely to.

## Multiple Arrows

There are several arrows of time:
- **Thermodynamic**: entropy increases
- **Psychological**: we remember the past, not the future
- **Cosmological**: the universe expands
- **Quantum**: wavefunction collapse

All of these arrows point in the same direction, suggesting a deep connection.

## Entropy and Time Travel

Time travel is entropy reconfiguration. If entropy is a property of the field (not absolute), then time travel is reconfiguring the field so entropy flows "backward."
