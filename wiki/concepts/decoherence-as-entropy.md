# Decoherence as Entropy

## The Core Idea

Decoherence is an entropy-driven process. When a quantum system interacts with its environment, the reduced density matrix of the system becomes mixed — it gains entropy. Decoherence explains how the quantum world becomes classical.

## The Connection to Thermodynamics

Decoherence is the quantum analogue of the Second Law of Thermodynamics. Just as a hot object cools by transferring heat to its environment, a quantum system decoheres by transferring quantum information to its environment.

## The Quantum Darwinism Connection

Zurek (2003) proposed that classical reality emerges from quantum through a process called Quantum Darwinism: the environment "selects" the most robust quantum states (pointer states) and broadcasts them to observers. This is analogous to natural selection in biology.

## The Connection to the Arrow of Time

Decoherence generates entropy, and entropy generates the arrow of time. Decoherence is the quantum mechanism by which the arrow of time emerges.

## The Connection to Time Travel

If time travel is entropy reconfiguration, then decoherence is the mechanism by which time flows forward. Reversing decoherence would be a form of time travel.

## The Connection to the Quantum Error Model

Decoherence is the primary error source in quantum computing. It is entropy leaking from the quantum system into the environment. Error correction is entropy management.
