# Wireless Energy

## Tesla Coil Theory

The Tesla coil is a resonant air-core transformer that produces high-voltage, high-frequency alternating current electricity.

| Parameter | Typical Values |
|-----------|---------------|
| Secondary voltage | 100 kV — 1 MV |
| Frequency | 50 kHz — 1 MHz |
| Power | 10 W — 300 kW |
| Coupling | Loose (k ≈ 0.1-0.2) |

Unlike conventional transformers, a Tesla coil uses **resonance** to achieve high voltage. The primary and secondary circuits are tuned to the same resonant frequency, and energy transfers between them over many cycles (not one cycle like a standard transformer).

The key physical principle: **electrostatic induction through a dielectric medium** (air). Energy is transmitted via the electric field, not through magnetic coupling alone.

## Wardenclyffe Tower (1901-1917)

Tesla's most ambitious wireless energy project:

- **Location:** Shoreham, Long Island
- **Height:** 187 feet (57 m)
- **Underground system:** 16 iron pipes driven 300 feet into the ground
- **Coil diameter:** 68 feet at the base
- **Power:** Designed for 300 kW transmission
- **Frequency:** ~150 kHz (not RF — tuned to Earth's Schumann resonance range)
- **Purpose:** Global wireless power distribution — "World Wireless System"

Tesla claimed the Earth itself is a conductor: "the Earth is like a big coil... turning with it, there is kinetic energy."
