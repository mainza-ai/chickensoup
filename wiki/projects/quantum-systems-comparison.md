# Quantum Systems Comparison

Comparison of quantum computing platforms for the time travel machinery.

## Comparison Table

| Platform | Best For | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Qiskit (IBM)** | Spacetime Engine | Most mature, 1000+ qubits, Qiskit Aer simulator | Limited qubit count for large simulations |
| **CUDA-Q (NVIDIA)** | Field Manipulator | Hybrid quantum-classical, GPU acceleration | Newer, less mature |
| **PennyLane** | AI Navigator | QML framework, differentiable quantum programming | Newer, smaller ecosystem |
| **D-Wave** | Optimization Backend | Quantum annealing, optimization | Limited to optimization problems |
| **IonQ** | Precision Backend | Trapped ion, highest fidelity | Limited qubit count, expensive |
| **Microsoft Q#** | Spacetime Engine | Topological qubits, Azure Quantum | Topological qubits still theoretical |
| **Google Cirq** | Spacetime Engine | Sycamore processor, quantum supremacy | Limited to superconducting qubits |

## Recommendation

- **Start with Qiskit** — most mature, best simulator
- **Add CUDA-Q** — hybrid quantum-classical
- **Use PennyLane** — QML for AI Navigator
- **D-Wave** — optimization backend
- **IonQ** — precision backend
