# Thomas Townsend Brown

Thomas Townsend Brown (1910–1985) was an American physicist and inventor best known for discovering the Biefeld-Brown effect (electrogravitics), a phenomenon where high-voltage asymmetric capacitors produce thrust in the direction of the smaller electrode.

## The Biefeld-Brown Effect

In the 1920s, while still a teenager, Brown and his mentor Paul Biefeld discovered that an asymmetric capacitor (one plate smaller than the other) in a high-voltage electric field experiences a force toward the smaller plate. This thrust cannot be fully explained by conventional ion wind and suggests a direct coupling between electricity and gravity.
