# Neil Turok

## Who

Neil Turok is a Canadian physicist, inaugural Higgs Chair at the University of Edinburgh, and former Director of the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics. Elected Fellow of the Royal Society in 2026.

## Key Work

Turok's recent work revives **quadratic gravity** (four-derivative gravity) as a viable theory of quantum gravity in four dimensions, challenging the consensus that quantum gravity requires strings or extra dimensions.

### Quadratic Gravity

- Generalizes Einstein-Hilbert action with curvature-squared terms (Ricci scalar squared + Weyl curvature squared)
- Renormalizable and asymptotically free (like QCD)
- Solves the Ostrogradsky instability by reinterpreting it as normal gravitational expansion
- Replaces Hilbert space with Krein space (generalization allowing negative norm states)
- Proposes a modified Born rule using projection operators that yields positive probabilities despite negative norm states

### CPT-Symmetric Universe

With Latham Boyle, Turok proposed a CPT-symmetric universe model: the universe before the Big Bang is the CPT mirror image of the universe after. This explains dark matter, spatial flatness, and the horizon puzzle without inflation.

### CMB as Quantum Gravity Signal

Turok argues that CMB fluctuations are direct evidence of quantum gravity — they follow the spectrum of a four-derivative field, exactly what quadratic gravity predicts.

## Significance

Turok represents a minimalist approach to fundamental physics: explaining observations with the simplest possible theory. His work challenges the string theory paradigm and offers a testable alternative.
