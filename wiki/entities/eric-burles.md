# Eric Burles

Rep. Eric Burles (R-MO) is a software engineer–turned–financial advisor–turned congressman. Since being elected to Congress in 2022, he has become one of the most prominent congressional advocates for UAP disclosure, serving on the House Oversight Committee.

## Background

- Software engineer; built one of the first patient portals in the U.S. (early 2000s)
- Consultant at Cerner and Oracle
- Studied finance and became a financial advisor
- Elected to Congress from Missouri in 2022, first term began in January 2023
- Listened to talk radio and economists (Milton Friedman, Thomas Sowell) before running; frustrated with DC politics, decided to run himself

## Entry into UAP Oversight

Burles became interested in UAPs shortly after being elected, when he saw David Grusch's public whistleblower interview. He reached out to Grusch's team and spoke with him the following week. Seeing Grusch — whom he described as a normal, credible person making extraordinary claims — inspired him to pursue UAP investigation as a congressional priority.

## UAP Disclosure Activities

- Authored interrogative letters to Aerospace Corporation, MIT Lincoln Labs, and MITRE Corporation seeking declassification
- Aerospace Corp interview turned up no information under Biden administration; intends to re-approach with more specific, detailed demands
- Current investigation centers on MITRE as an FFRDC potentially embedded in UFO programs
- Working to secure White House–level briefing on UAP programs (never granted to prior lawmakers such as Barry Goldwater)
- Traveled to Patuxent River (PAX River), the Navy's experimental aircraft base, after a request made during a 2 a.m. budget negotiation session

## Key Claims and Events

### Western U.S. Military UAP Event
Burles claims he was the first elected official briefed on a recent UAP event at a western U.S. military installation, where intelligence community personnel on the ground physically encountered objects:

- Glowing orbs described as "plasmoids" — roughly 3 meters in diameter, in multiple colors
- Objects interacted with a helicopter, then chased F-16s that were scrambled to intercept
- Intel officials elevated the event from below priority 500 to a top-10 government priority
- Event is partially referenced in recent ODNI releases (details not formally declassified)

### MIT Lincoln Labs Audio
- A 1952 audio recording exists of a senior Department of Defense official briefing MIT scientists about "saucers"
- Burles issued an interrogative letter demanding its retention and release
- MIT Lincoln Labs confirmed the file exists and is processing it for release via SCIF

### Matthew Sullivan Death
- Matthew Sullivan was a UAP program insider with firsthand craft experience who died two weeks before he was scheduled to brief Congress
- Burles and Grusch co-authored a letter to the FBI requesting an investigation through the Oversight Committee
- The letter was never received during the Biden administration and had to be resubmitted
- Grush was shaken by Sullivan's death

### Hybrid Programs Allegations
- Multiple individuals have briefed Burles on secret DoD programs involving genetic hybridization between humans and non-human entities
- Claims are second-hand (briefings to his office), not personally witnessed or independently verified
- Burles consistently stresses he does not personally believe these accounts; he is merely relaying what was told to him

### Tim Taylor / Aerospace Corporation
- Tim Taylor (allegedly a NASA mission controller also affiliated with Aerospace Corporation) has claimed involvement in a secret space program
- Appeared in *American Cosmic* (Diana Pasulka); claims to "adjust timelines"
- Showed Stanford professor Gary Nolan a crash site with anomalous material
- Burles interviewed Taylor at a 2003 MITRE conference in Washington, DC, focused on high-frequency gravitational waves

### Lou Elizondo
- Describes Alizondo as a patriot bound by NDAs, unable to confirm or deny involvement in special access programs like Immaculate Constellation
- Believes Elizondo wants to disclose more but must follow legal processes (DOD pre-publication review)

### Whistleblower and Physical Threats
- Multiple whistleblowers (including an unnamed special forces veteran) have warned Burles about private-sector actors conducting "kinetic activity" against UAP investigators
- Burles does not live in fear, citing Psalm 91 ("God's timing"); notes more death threats from unions over right-to-work legislation than from UAP topic

### Psyionics / Consciousness-Based UAP Contact
- Attended an informal UAP community gathering in Washington, DC
- Chris Bledsoe (a UAP witness/contactee) claimed he could call in UAPs; agreed to demonstrate on the Capitol lawn with Burles present
- No UAP appeared during the Capitol lawn demonstration

## Political Stance

- Self-described as a free-market capitalist, fiscal conservative
- Believes UAP disclosure is not a partisan issue: "This is about the nature of reality. No government should have a monopoly on that"
- Sees disclosure as empowering academic inquiry; opposes government withholding knowledge about fundamental physics
- Uses his committee role to crowdsource UFO research tips from the public, then sends targeted letters to agencies
- Has met with international counterparts (Japan, Canada, Brazil) pursuing their own UAP investigations

## Key Quotes

- "If we discover that our government has held on to antigravitic information from the scientific community, I'm going to be really pissed."
- "I don't live my life looking over my shoulder. If it's my time and God's ready to take me home, then that's his timing."
- "This is a watershed moment... this topic is not something that's going to advance you politically. People are going to smirk about it and you won't be taken seriously. I think it ended up working."
