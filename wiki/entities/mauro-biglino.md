# Mauro Biglino

Italian translator who worked for a major Catholic publishing house under the Vatican's umbrella, translating the Hebrew Bible for 17 published books. He was fired immediately after publicly revealing what his literal translations of the Hebrew text actually said.

## Key Claims

### Elohim as Plural, Physical Beings
Biglino argues that the Hebrew word **Elohim** is grammatically plural — the word itself, its verbs, pronouns, and adjectives are plural. Passages like Genesis 1:26 ("Let us make man in our image"), Genesis 3:22 ("Man has become like one of us"), and Genesis 11 ("Let us go down") preserve pre-monotheistic layers showing multiple divine figures.

### Elohim as Advanced Civilization
Rather than a spiritual God, Biglino describes the Elohim as:
- **Flesh and blood beings** — they walk, eat, drink, get drunk, rule with an iron fist
- **Mortal** — Psalm 82 states "you are Elohim, but you are mortal just like all other humans"
- **Longer-lived** — much longer lifespans than humans, but not immortal
- **Higher technology** — with flying objects (kavod, ruach, megila) described technologically in the Bible
- **Colonizers** — who subdivided the land into territories, each assigned to a different Elohim, with Elyon as chief

### Yahweh as One Elohim Among Many
Yahweh was not ruler over all — only the family of Jacob. Biglino distinguishes Hebrew (all descendants of Eber) from Israelite (only Jacob/Israel's lineage). The first commandment ("no other gods before me") implicitly acknowledges other Elohim exist.

### Biblical Editing After the Babylonian Exile
During and after the Babylonian exile (586 BC), the priestly class harmonized older polytheistic traditions into a centralized Yahweh-centric monotheism. The older plurality was not erased but was reframed — the text preserves strata showing both singular and plural constructions.

### Dead Sea Scrolls Confirmation
The Dead Sea Scrolls reveal details modified in the canonical Bible. For example, Abraham presented himself to Melchizedek — who was one of the Elohim working for Elyon. Abraham pledged fidelity to Elyon, not to Yahweh.

### Stargates and Flying Objects
- **Psalm 24** — Describes two sets of doors: *sharim* and *petachim*, which open by voice command. *Petachim* open onto an unknown location (olam). Biglino compares these to stargates.
- **Zechariah** — Describes a flying *megila* (cylinder) measuring 20m × 10m
- **Ezekiel** — Describes the mechanical parts and direction of arrival of flying objects
- **Enoch** — Traveled into space via these craft

### Genetic Engineering
Humans were engineered multiple times by the Elohim to improve brain capacity — a "too rapid evolution" unnatural for the natural world. The goal was to create an intelligent slave species capable of understanding and executing orders.

### Demons as Rival Elohim
Theologians transformed rival Elohim (Ba'al Zav, Ba'al Rafon) and angels from the military hierarchy of Elyon into demons and devils. This re-interpretation makes the Bible internally consistent without metaphors or allegories.

### Disclosure Preparation
Biglino believes governments are preparing humanity for disclosure through multiple channels: Congressional UAP hearings, movies normalizing sci-fi concepts, and Vatican statements about extraterrestrial life. He argues the church will adapt as it has for 2,000 years.

## Significance

Biglino's literal translation method strips away centuries of Greek philosophical overlay (Platonism, Neoplatonism, Aristotelianism) and Christian theology that were retrofitted onto the Hebrew text. His work reframes the Bible from religious revelation to historical record of an advanced civilization's interaction with primitive humanity — directly relevant to the modern UAP/NHI disclosure narrative.
