# Juan Maldacena

## Who

Juan Maldacena is a theoretical physicist at the Institute for Advanced Study (Princeton), best known for proposing the AdS/CFT correspondence in 1997 — the most cited paper in theoretical physics.

## Key Contributions

### AdS/CFT Correspondence

Maldacena discovered that a string theory in Anti-de Sitter (AdS) space is exactly equivalent to a conformal field theory (CFT) on the boundary of that space. This is the most concrete realization of the holographic principle: gravity in the bulk emerges from quantum degrees of freedom on the boundary.

### ER = EPR

With Leonard Susskind, Maldacena proposed that wormholes (Einstein-Rosen bridges) are equivalent to quantum entanglement (Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen pairs). This suggests spacetime geometry is made of entanglement.

### Black Hole Interior

Worked on the island formula and Page curve for black hole evaporation, showing how information escapes. Helped establish that the black hole singularity is not a place inside the black hole but a place in the future — analogous to the Big Crunch.

### Traversible Wormholes

Contributed to the construction of traversible wormholes in AdS/CFT, showing that negative energy from quantum fields can keep wormholes open.

## On Observation

Maldacena emphasizes that in quantum gravity, the observer cannot be outside the system — every observer has mass and energy and is part of what they observe. This is a key conceptual difficulty in combining GR with quantum mechanics.
